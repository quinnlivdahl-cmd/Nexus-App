# Legacy Worlds use literal calendar chronology

Each Legacy World owns one deterministic World Calendar that assigns canonical dates or date ranges to campaign starts, Campaign Resolutions, Saga Sources, and Legacy Consequences so future lore can reason about ordering and elapsed time. The calendar is locally authoritative rather than model-owned: models may propose suitable durations or constraints, but deterministic rules validate ordering, normalize representation, and own advancement. Literal lore chronology coexists with abstract Campaign Clocks and consequence-focused Route Prospects; abstract route-duration categories may advance the calendar without requiring the player to manage distance or travel-time arithmetic.

ADR 0062 sets campaign-start timing: the CM proposes an exact date after the Player Character and Starting Crew are known, and deterministic validation protects chronology and selected-character availability.

ADR 0066 separates World Calendar age from Biological Age and excludes long-Transit Stasis from material biological aging.
