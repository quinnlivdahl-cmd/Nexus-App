# Surviving Player Characters become Archive Crewmates

A surviving Player Character from a completed campaign automatically converts into an eligible Crew Archive character, while Player Character Permanent Loss prevents conversion. In later campaigns the converted character occupies an ordinary Crewmate role and never replaces the newly created Player Character as group leader. Conversion preserves identity, Personal Canon, authored build foundations, Aptitude Traits, applicable Archive Memories, and other explicitly persistent state. The converted character retains the current committed Embodiment they had at campaign end, including its appearance, all validated Installed Cyberware added at creation or during the campaign, resulting Chassis tier, and validated durable gene modifications. Ordinary carried equipment and loadout, run-specific level, temporary upgrades, Ability ranks, Skill Focus investment, cyberware upgrade-tree investment, and other run-specific Ability development reset or rebase into the universal Level-0 Campaign Build; the Embodiment and persistent physical modifications themselves are not removed by that reset.

Conversion produces one capped Converted Crewmate Package rather than copying the original Level-0 sheet or stacking an extra trait layer on top of it. The normal Player Character Creation spend allowance is its target. Its traits, starting Abilities, Installed Cyberware, durable gene modifications, and other build selections are chosen from source-referenced campaign evidence to best represent who the character was at campaign end. The package may select authored Campaign-Earned Traits that are unavailable during normal Player Character Creation when validated campaign evidence satisfies their unlock requirements, but each selected trait is priced inside the same allowance rather than granted as free carryover. Growth Traits may supply evidence but do not themselves persist. The Crew Profile and mechanical package may differ from the character's original Level-0 choices while remaining a legal complete build for its entry level. Mandatory persistent implants and durable gene modifications are costed before discretionary selections. If they alone exceed the normal creation allowance, a Legacy Allowance records and covers only that unavoidable overage; it grants no discretionary points and preserves every associated drawback, maintenance need, compatibility limit, hack surface, or other validated cost.

A former Player Character whose mandatory persistent modifications exceed the normal Level-0 creation allowance remains a valid Crew Archive character but is not eligible for Starting Crew, and Legacy Allowance cannot bypass that opening restriction. Once the campaign's current level can support a legal level-appropriate version of the Converted Crewmate Package, the character enters the Campaign Director's eligible recruitment candidate pool rather than receiving a guaranteed appearance. Any Legacy Allowance at that point still covers only irreducible mandatory overage. The Crew Profile derives from custom dialogue, decisions, relationships, repeated behavior, and prior development rather than model invention, and future context uses the validated profile and targeted recall instead of loading raw campaign transcripts.

At Campaign Resolution, the system presents an evidence-backed default Converted Crewmate Package for player review. The player may adjust discretionary traits and starting Abilities only among options unlocked by validated evidence and only within the capped package allowance. Installed Cyberware, durable gene modifications, resulting Chassis, and other persistent physical facts are fixed by committed state rather than editable during this review. Finalization validates and commits one legal package; the review cannot rewrite campaign history or grant unsupported options.

ADR 0071 resets converted characters to the same complete level-0 campaign baseline used by all starting characters.
