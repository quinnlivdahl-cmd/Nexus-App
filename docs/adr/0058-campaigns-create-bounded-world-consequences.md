# Campaigns create bounded world consequences

Campaigns are stories within the shared setting rather than vehicles for erasing its foundational factions or resolving its defining conflicts. Campaign Resolution promotes compact Legacy Consequences that describe how the world is now different—such as changes to faction leadership or influence, regional control, relationships, public knowledge, institutions, resources, or operations—while procedural details remain secondary history. Main Campaign Threads and Playable Finales target campaign-scale problems, faction arms, leaders, operations, or regional conditions; Setting Pillars persist in changed form so later campaigns inherit meaningful consequences without losing the world that supports future play.

ADR 0059 clarifies that this bounded-consequence rule does not restrict campaigns to local geography; a campaign may be a system-wide adventure while leaving specific durable changes.
