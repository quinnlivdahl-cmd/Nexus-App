# Node 10 hosts the Playable Finale and Campaign Resolution follows

The last player-ordered member of the Climax Set becomes the Finale Node and hosts the Playable Finale: the campaign's final major objective or operation. It may be a confrontation, rescue, sabotage, escape, negotiation, discovery, decision, or another culmination rather than a mandatory literal showdown. Deterministic mechanics and validated outcomes resolve the Playable Finale and commit Node 10 Game Truth. Campaign Resolution is a separate post-node phase that may use generated narration or epilogue to express the committed aftermath, close or record remaining Campaign Threads, and preserve campaign history; it cannot decide the outcome, erase prior consequences, or substitute narration for the playable climax.

ADR 0055 defines Campaign Resolution as a validated structured closure call followed by a separate epilogue-performance call.
