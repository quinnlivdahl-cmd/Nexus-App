# Campaign pressure creates non-obvious consequential Route Choices

The Campaign Director maintains bounded Route Pressure that coaxes the Main Campaign Thread forward even when the player prioritizes other opportunities, but deterministic Campaign Clocks and state services own actual advancement and consequences. Every Route Prospect offers a distinct meaningful incentive and Route Opportunity Cost, and several or all prospects may develop the Main Campaign Thread differently without exposing a normal-player `story option` label. Pressure developments must cite an existing source, trigger, clock, and player-visible signal; they may reshape, worsen, expire, or create opportunities but cannot invent retroactive facts, impose unforeseeable punishment, eliminate all meaningful alternatives, or turn route selection into one obviously correct choice.

ADR 0050 sets the normal prospect lifecycle: unchosen offers lapse, and an exceptional resurfacing must return as a materially changed linked offer rather than a repeated static option.
