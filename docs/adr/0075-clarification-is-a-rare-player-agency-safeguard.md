# Clarification is a rare player-agency safeguard

Nexus interprets plausible Player Intent flexibly and prefers a legal rules-native reading over rejection or repeated clarification. Clarification occurs only when materially different interpretations would change the actor, target, cost, Check, immediate risk, or authorized outcome and the system cannot safely infer the player's meaning. When that ambiguity forms a reliable finite set, Nexus presents explicit interpretations with player-known mechanical terms; otherwise it asks one focused freeform question. If the exact action is impossible but its goal has a nearby plausible alternative grounded in visible Game Truth, Nexus may offer that alternative with its materially different known costs or risks, but never executes it without confirmation. These options are an exceptional safeguard, not a replacement for the Intent Bar or a funnel toward predefined actions: unusual plans that can be expressed through existing rules and authoritative state remain valid Freeform Actions, and nothing commits while clarification or alternative confirmation is pending.
