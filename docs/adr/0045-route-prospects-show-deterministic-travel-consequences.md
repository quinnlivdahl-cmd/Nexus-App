# Route Prospects show deterministic Travel Consequences

Each Route Prospect shows the concrete Travel Consequences of choosing it rather than requiring literal distance or calendar duration. The Campaign Director may propose the destination and route reference, but deterministic travel rules derive consequences from an abstract route-duration category, the player Ship's baseline, Navigation capability, relevant Ship Conditions, and active campaign clocks. Consequences may advance deadlines, faction activity, Campaign Threads, or other Route Pressure, complete recovery, or alter Transit Event exposure. Calendar language may remain fiction unless an explicit authored scenario or validated clock makes it mechanical, and Lore Ship Classes do not silently modify player travel rules.

ADR 0061 adds a literal World Calendar for lore chronology. Deterministic route-duration mapping may advance that calendar without changing this ADR's player-facing emphasis on consequences over travel math.
