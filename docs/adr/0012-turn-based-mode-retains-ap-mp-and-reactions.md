# Turn-Based Mode retains AP, MP, and reactions

The current action-economy split survives: a standard activation begins with two Action Points for deliberate tactical actions, Movement Points represent continuous navigable path distance, reactions provide limited triggered responses outside a normal activation, and one free Micro-Interaction handles genuinely small environmental or item actions. A character normally takes no more than one offensive action per activation so the second AP supports tactical setup, defense, interaction, or movement; explicit features may break that limit. Node-to-node movement is removed, Dash-like actions may convert Action Points into additional movement, and no AP or MP accounting applies during Free Movement.
