# Humanity, Posthumanity, and Origin Frame

## Grounding rule

Nexus does not use fantasy race logic as its default. Most peoples are better understood as human-derived peoples, body states, legal categories, technological lineages, political identities, or contact-altered conditions.

Origin and body categories should remain grounded in plausible or at least internally consistent human divergence: biotechnology, cybernetics, upload continuity, AI personhood, artificial bodies, habitat adaptation, and Signal effects.

## Active frame

The important question is not "what species are they?" The important question is what institution, body, memory, labor contract, legal regime, or technology is claiming continuity with a human person.

`Characters` owns character-creation chassis and playability. `Lore` owns lore framing, legal and social conflict, and personhood stakes.

## Current broad groups

### Organic and near-baseline humans

Organic and near-baseline humans must remain narratively and mechanically interesting. Their strengths can include lower hack surface, wider legal recognition, cultural legitimacy, lower maintenance dependence, and resistance to coercive implant networks. They are not obsolete peasants by default.

### Gene-edited humans

The current working trio of gene-edited human lore categories is:

1. **Designer Humans** - elite, aristocratic, family-line, or status-line humans with curated signature traits, inherited presentation, controlled lineage aesthetics, and prestige biology. Their social meaning is tied to family, patronage, name, appearance, and recognition.
2. **Vatborn** - grown or artificial-birth humans tied to designed purpose, labor, contract, institutional control, quasi-property history, or caste/status pressure. Vatborn are usually Standard Fit, but their legal/social position may be contested.
3. **Low-G / Adapted Humans** - the recovered third gene-edited human branch. This group is preserved as a habitat-adaptation, low-gravity, or environmental adaptation branch, but the final term and mechanics remain open.

These are lore categories, not locked character packages. `Characters` owns the character-facing trait hooks and balance implications.

### Vatborn lore pressure

Vatborn should be understood as human, not manufactured monsters or fantasy clones. Their conflict comes from how institutions categorize their birth, purpose, ownership, contract, caste, and independence.

Low Caste is a social/legal/cultural pressure, not a universal incompetence trait. It may make elites ignore a Vatborn, discount testimony, doubt authority, treat them as assigned-function labor, or assume they belong in service spaces. The same pressure may sometimes help them move through back-of-house areas, blend into labor networks, or hear what high-status people say when they think no equal is present.

#### Creator marks and proprietary body interfaces

Many Vatborn lines carry patterned pigmentation added during artificial development by their creator institution. These patterns are actual skin color—genetically directed or otherwise biologically laid down—not tattoos applied after birth. They may identify the creator, production lineage, assigned function, contract class, or other status the institution intended the body to advertise. Marks may cross the scalp, face, throat, arms, hands, or legs so that the creator's claim remains difficult to conceal.

Creator institutions often design a Vatborn line and its supporting technology together. Company-issued cyberware may therefore use proprietary interfaces tuned to that line's engineered biology. The result can be more elegant, intimate, and reliable than generic augmentation while also creating dependence on maker-approved clinics, parts, firmware, or specialized black markets.

Creator marks persist after emancipation, escape, contract transfer, corporate collapse, or legal recognition as an independent person. Vatborn may hide, alter, deface, reclaim, decorate, or deliberately display them. A creator mark records what an institution intended to own; it does not establish that the marked person remains property.

The Anti-Uprising Frame remains unresolved: it may be universal, common, line-specific, propaganda, optional background, or false assumption. Do not lock it as default without later review.

### Splicers

Splicers are separate from gene-edited human categories. They are biologically altered or uplifted human-derived/animal-derived peoples whose lore should stay tied to labor, survival, environment, medicine, reproduction, habitat access, and legal status rather than becoming fantasy species.

Important preserved corrections:

- Ape splicers were first.
- Apes are apes made human, not humans made ape.
- Lizard people were bred for long space flight.
- Mycelial/mushroom people are less settled.
- Cephalopod people are newer and less settled.
- Mycelial and cephalopod concepts involve more difficult invertebrate/non-mammal biological blending and should not be treated as settled default populations yet.

Splicers are not Standard Fit by default, though particular lines, adaptations, or custom gear may reduce that friction.

### Cyborgs and cybernetic persons

Cyborg identity should not default to a separate species pick. `Characters` corrected the active character model: cybernetic integration is normally derived from cyberware chosen during character creation and from the role cyberware plays in survival, labor, perception, mobility, combat, or identity.

Lore questions include repair rights, repossession, firmware control, remote disablement, sensor-log evidence, cybernetic disability politics, debt-bound prosthetics, and implant surveillance.

Full-chassis cyborgs remain distinct from uploads. A full-chassis cyborg can still have a biological brain/person-core in a mostly mechanical body.

### Upload persons

Upload persons raise questions of continuity, copying, death, consent, legal recognition, forks, memory edits, inheritance, testimony, and bodily embodiment. The personhood question belongs here and in `Characters`; server infrastructure and polity scale belong in the faction and polity section.

### AI persons and AI systems

AI may appear as tools, corporate infrastructure, ship systems, legal agents, crew candidates, or persons. Not every AI is a person and not every person-like AI is legally recognized. AI should remain part of the personhood conflict rather than an easy background utility.

### Signal-touched conditions

Signal-touched status should usually be an overlay or condition, not a simple species category. It may affect cognition, perception, Contact capability, dreams, synchronization, compulsion, or anomaly interaction. It remains unresolved whether this is contact, contamination, emergent network behavior, alien physics, or human misinterpretation.

## Design caution

Do not flatten the setting into a list of playable races. Keep origin, body, legal identity, vulnerability, access, and social treatment linked.

## Cross-slot boundary for origins

- `Characters` owns playable character origin and chassis implications, trait hooks, Standard Fit as a character-facing compatibility tag, and body-fit mechanics.
- `Equipment` owns cyberware pieces, integrated tools, EMP and cyber-disruption, and enhancement modules.
- `Lore` owns full lore, history, law, caste, faction ideology, personhood arguments, and social meaning.
