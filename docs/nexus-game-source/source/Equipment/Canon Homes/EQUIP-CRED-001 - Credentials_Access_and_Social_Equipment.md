# Credentials Access and Social Equipment

## 1. Credentials are not slotted

Credentials are not loadout-slot items by default.

Current rule:

```text
Credentials are stored as acquired.
If the crew has the credential, they have it, unless revoked, expired, spoofed, traced, contested, or fictionally compromised.
```

Credentials can be physical, digital, legal, social, factional, forged, hacked, inherited, purchased, stolen, negotiated, or story-granted.

## 2. What credentials do

Credentials may:

- grant access to rooms, station systems, areas, transit, cargo, storage, clinics, markets, or data;
- grant permission to carry or use restricted gear;
- reduce suspicion;
- reveal social or faction options;
- allow requisition from shared armory, faction store, black clinic, or corporate supply;
- create legal/custody consequences when abused.

## 3. Social equipment

Social equipment is gear whose main value is access, deception, presentation, proof, procedure, surveillance, blackmail, or faction interaction.

Examples from playtest/handoffs include:

- custody gear;
- coats/gloves/veils;
- access card;
- transfer chit;
- beacon/token remnants;
- observer equipment;
- consent check cards;
- recorder patches;
- forged credentials;
- disguises;
- legal/custody tags.

These are examples, not a universal catalog.

## 4. Spoofed credentials and planning

Nexus must support avoiding fights through:

- stealth;
- social play;
- bribery;
- blackmail;
- hacking;
- spoofed credentials;
- route discovery;
- distractions;
- patrol rerouting;
- faction procedure.

Credentials should therefore affect both skill-revealed options and mission risk.

## 5. Failure and compromise

Credential failure does not always mean immediate combat.

Possible consequences:

- suspicion clock increases;
- access is delayed;
- escort is required;
- false clearance works once but leaves trace;
- item is confiscated;
- faction status changes;
- objective path closes;
- social or legal complication begins;
- NetSec or station security gets a signal.

## 6. Mass-intake clarifications

### 7.1 Credentials as equipment permissions

Credentials are not slotted by default, but they can function as equipment permissions. They may permit or influence:

- restricted gear;
- custody tools;
- access to markets;
- black clinics;
- faction armories;
- station systems;
- legal carry;
- checkpoint passage;
- salvage authority;
- medical or repair access.

### 7.2 Credential failure model

Credential failure should often create suspicion, trace, escort, delay, confiscation, price increase, legal complication, or soft lock rather than instantly starting combat.

### 7.3 Campaign-specific items

Named campaign items such as access cards, transfer chits, observer equipment, recorder patches, beacon remnants, token remnants, and custody gear should route to `Dashboards` or later campaign-state surfaces unless generalized into reusable equipment rules.
