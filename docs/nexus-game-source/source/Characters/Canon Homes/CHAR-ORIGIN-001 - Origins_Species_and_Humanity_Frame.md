# Origins, Species, and Humanity Frame

## 1. Operating language

The filename preserves the Slot Map term `Species`, but the active Nexus language should be:

```text
Origins / Chassis / Humanity Frame
```

The project theme rejects fantasy-race assumptions. Character origins are body states, legal categories, technological lineages, cultural labels, and arguments about personhood.

## 2. Taxonomy correction

Older lore source uses major families such as Organic Human, Splicer, Cyborg, Upload, AI, and Signal-Touched. That remains useful as a lore/search taxonomy, but character creation should not treat every family as a fixed chassis pick.

Current character-creation correction:

- Organic / near-baseline human can be a starting body/origin.
- Splicer can be a starting body/origin if allowed by the campaign.
- Upload can be a starting embodiment path if the campaign supports it.
- AI and Signal-Touched remain special/restricted until their player-facing model is developed.
- Cyborg usually emerges from cyberware investment, not a separate default starting body type.

## 3. Cybernetic identity and humanity frame

Cybernetic status is a sliding integration result.

Minor implants do not automatically make someone a Cyborg in the strong social or mechanical sense. A person may have medical implants, communication chips, occupational tools, or background augmentation while still being treated as organic, baseline, or near-baseline by many institutions.

A high-integration character may become legally, socially, or mechanically treated as Cyborg, especially at Tier 2 and above.

## 4. Splice + cyber rarity

Splice characters may select cyberware, but the combination should not become the free best build.

Splice + cyber PCs are allowed as special cases, but they are rare in lore and should carry drawbacks such as:

```text
higher creation-point cost
special clinic requirement
bespoke modules
biological rejection risk
limited module capacity
gene-cyber instability
rare faction permission
medical/repair complexity
Firewall/hack-surface consequences
possible weakening of splice/body advantages
```

## 5. Full Chassis Cyborg vs Upload

A Full Chassis Cyborg is not an Upload.

```text
Full Chassis Cyborg = biological brain/person-core in a mostly mechanical body.
Upload = digital/person-substrate existence embodied through sleeve, drone, shell, server, or other substrate.
```

Chassis damage can often be repaired or replaced. Brain/person-core damage is much more serious. A simple chassis swap is not clean resurrection unless special preservation technology exists.

## 6. Upload starting bodies

Accepted upload starting embodiment options:

| Upload body | Meaning | Likely use |
|---|---|---|
| Base Sleeve | Humanoid/socially legible body. | Flexible play, social access, normal gear use, personhood presentation. |
| Maintenance Drone | Utility/repair/ship-system body. | Engineering, access ducts, repair, tools, low-social presence. |
| Combat Shell | Hardened tactical body. | Durability, weapons, high threat/legal/security attention. |

Upload body choice affects Defense, Armor, HP, Firewall, System Integrity, gear compatibility, social access, repair needs, continuity risks, legal status, body replacement options, and upgrade trees.

## 7. DM Mode safeguards

DM Mode may use these as tags and affordances, not fixed species stat blocks. Whenever a body gives a major advantage, give it a visible tradeoff, cost, exposure, legality issue, environmental limit, or relationship pressure.

## 8. Source handling note

This doc reconciles older lore-family language with newer Seed handoff corrections. Lore taxonomy and faction/legal implications should be deepened in `Lore`.

## 9. Character-facing origin update and lore split

This doc should not absorb the full lore of gene-editing, splicer history, caste, faction ideology, personhood law, or "what counts as human" politics. `Characters` owns character-facing origin/chassis implications. `Lore` owns full lore.

### 9.1 Character-facing origin buckets

Use these as current character-facing buckets, not a locked playable list:

| Bucket | Characters use |
|---|---|
| Near-baseline / Organic Human | Flexible Standard Fit default and broad social/equipment compatibility. |
| Designer Human | Standard Fit gene-edited human with possible family-line, elite, optimization, expectation, or access hooks. Full aristocratic/family-line culture routes to `Lore`. |
| Vatborn | Standard Fit engineered human. Candidate hooks: Engineered Focus and Low Caste. Full caste/social history routes to `Lore`. |
| Low-G / Adapted Human | Likely Standard Fit unless a later trait says otherwise. Candidate hooks should focus on low-gravity adaptation, fragility/strength tradeoffs, movement, endurance, or environment. Full settlement/culture history routes to `Lore`. |
| Splicer | Not Standard Fit by default. Character-facing hooks are body affordances, infrastructure friction, medical complexity, social risk, and map-facing body fit. Species-line history routes to `Lore`. |
| Cybernetically integrated character | Defined by selected cyberware and resulting integration tier, not a default starting species. Equipment/cyberware details route to `Equipment`. |
| Upload / AI / Signal-Touched | Restricted or campaign-specific until player-facing models mature. Personhood law and full lore route to `Lore`. |

### 9.2 Vatborn trait hooks without full caste doctrine

Current `Characters` frame:

```text
Vatborn = Standard Fit + Engineered Focus + Low Caste
```

This is a character hook frame, not final mechanics.

- **Engineered Focus:** candidate resistance to panic, intimidation, distraction, and ordinary emotional destabilization.
- **Low Caste:** situational social-position hook, not a flat penalty to all social checks.

Low Caste should affect contexts where authority, credibility, independence, station, testimony, and elite recognition matter. It may also help in service-role camouflage, being underestimated, or moving through low-status spaces. `Lore` should own the setting explanation.

### 9.3 Splicer lore boundary

Character-facing splicer entries may mention only the play implications needed at the table: body affordances, Standard Fit problems, medical/recovery concerns, prejudice/risk, and map/infrastructure consequences.

Detailed splicer history, including which lines came first and why, belongs in `Lore`.

### 9.4 C-POH and cyberware boundary

`Characters` should not define C-POH ideology in detail. Character-facing note only:

- some political, religious, legal, or faction groups may treat mind-altering/personhood-invasive implants differently from restorative prosthetics, body-assist systems, or integrated occupational tools;
- exact doctrine and law route to `Lore`;
- specific cyberware, EMP, system-disruption, and module rules route to `Equipment`.
