## Nexus Constraints

The attached package establishes several design constraints that should dominate system choice. Nexus is tabletop-first; it wants polished GPT-DM readability; TacMaps are node-webs rather than square grids; AP/MP/reaction are already visible but explicitly provisional; movement and objective interaction must matter; broad skills with focuses/tags/traits are preferred; and the core build principle remains **Bodies bias. Builds decide.** The package also makes clear that the paused mechanics branch stalled because powers had nothing stable to modify yet.

| Constraint from the attached package | Surface-level consequence |
|---|---|
| Tabletop-first, polished DM mode | Prefer compact track sets, reusable verbs, and condition families over simulation-heavy exception stacks |
| Node-web TacMaps with MP and route tags | Translate distance into route cost, exposure, line, vertical access, and node state |
| AP/MP/reaction display already exists | Favor action models that can survive that display, at least for v0.1 playtests |
| Movement and objectives must matter | Do not let attack economy swallow interact, carry, hack, or rescue play |
| Bodies bias, builds decide | Put major identity in skills, focuses, tags, gear, mods, and crew tools, not body-locked trees |
| Broad skills plus specialties/tags/traits | Use fewer ranked domains, then let focuses and tags do the expressive work |
| Legal/faction should stay light | Model it through credentials, permits, warrants, and standing tags, not a parallel subsystem |
| Package is context, not canon | Recommendations should stay provisional and reversible |

The attached package also justifies a medium-density surface map rather than a maximal rules chassis. Combat will carry more of the play load, but noncombat must still have concrete hooks. That points toward a design where the tactical layer has the most explicit surfaces, while secondary campaign systems stay short, legible, and modular.

That scaffold implies a later character sheet rhythm like this:

- **Core skill domain:** ranked competence; broad, visible, long-lived
- **Focus:** narrower specialty that grants permissions, discounts, or unlocks
- **Technique/power:** active or rule-bending mechanical effect
- **Trait/tag:** body, background, credential, faction, cyberware, or event-based conditional permission

That distinction matters because Nexus wants skills to do more than add roll bonuses. In the attached package, skills are already expected to reveal routes, reduce AP/MP costs, improve Dash/Sprint, resist displacement, reveal risks, and unlock traversal or objective methods. Those are **permission verbs**, not just math verbs.

The current working list is therefore best handled with these judgment calls:

| Current working item | Best future home | Recommendation |

| **Security** | Infiltration plus Computing | Split physical breach from digital intrusion rather than making one overloaded skill |
| **Adaptation** | Survival / Biotech / trait lane | Too broad as a standalone skill unless Nexus wants a major hazard-body lane |
| **Culture** | Tag/focus lane | Better as knowledge/credential modifiers than as a universal progression tax |

## Math Surface and Power Hooks

The attached package is correct that Nexus does not need final math yet; it needs **surfaces and tuning knobs**. A good rule of thumb for v0.1 is that early powers should bend **one surface** well, mid-tier powers should convert **one surface into another**, and signature powers should rewrite **the relationship between surfaces**. That produces much more interesting build expression than flat stacking.

| **Objectives** | faster upload, carry without tax, rescue without full action, scan reveal, plant/disarm charge, evidence protection |

The best math surface is therefore not one final formula. It is a stable list of things powers can modify: **action cost, route cost, clock progress, status severity, resource gain/loss, shield/armor state, wound state, access/trace state, and objective authority**.
