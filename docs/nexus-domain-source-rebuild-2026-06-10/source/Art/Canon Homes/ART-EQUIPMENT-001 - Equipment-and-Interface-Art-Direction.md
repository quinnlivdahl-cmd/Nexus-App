# Equipment and Interface Art Direction

## Equipment design principle

Equipment should look like it was built for fragile habitats, legal constraints, maintenance access, and field repair. Visual design should imply consequences: recoil, seal damage, heat, noise, legality, hack surface, oxygen risk, or repair burden.

## Weapons

Nexus weapons are not generic fantasy weapons. Show:

- compact habitat-safe forms where appropriate;
- recoil management and bracing;
- heat sinks, warning tags, and sealed magazines;
- corporate/state serial markings;
- improvised modifications;
- frangible, low-penetration, foam, shock, or tool-like options where source supports them.

Do not finalize damage, categories, or weapon lists through art.

## Protective gear

Protective gear is the equipped suit, armor, plating, or defensive shell. Armor/Armor Rating is a value in rules, not necessarily a separate object.

Visual cues may include:

- pressure-rated suit layers;
- hard plates over soft seals;
- shield emitters or defense modules;
- patch kits and warning sensors;
- emergency sealant ports;
- station safety markings;
- heat/radiation/vacuum tags.

## Tools

Tools are major loadout anchors. They should look action-enabling rather than decorative:

- hacking deck or security spike;
- fabrication kit;
- med rig;
- survey scanner;
- engineering clamp pack;
- drone controller;
- breaching or seal tool;
- social/credential kit.

A tool may imply permissions, tags, actions, cooldowns, or access, but the rules source owns those effects.

## Accessories and kits

Accessories are discrete items or small themed kits. Visuals should support quick recognition:

- forgery kit;
- cold-case pouch;
- sensor puck;
- trauma patch set;
- spare sealant;
- flare beacon;
- evidence bag;
- recorder patch.

## Credentials

Credentials are not slotted gear, but they can be visualized through badges, chips, access cards, tokens, encrypted marks, or temporary passes. Credential art should show social power and revocation risk.

## Cyberware and bioware

Cyberware art should show integration, maintenance, vulnerability, and hack surface:

- visible ports and cable routing;
- diagnostic LEDs;
- panel seams;
- clinic tags;
- heat venting;
- corrupted interface alerts;
- nerve-interface scars;
- upgrade nodes as body/system modules.

Bioware should feel medical and adapted rather than purely monstrous.

## Interface art

Interfaces should be readable and functional:

- terminal panels;
- physical switches;
- small displays;
- layered alert strips;
- flat tactical overlays;
- damaged or outdated UI;
- status colors used sparingly.

Interface visuals should support DM/player understanding and should not bury key information in decorative noise.
## Equipment, interface, and system-surface update - 2026-05-15

### System Status visual support

Art and iconography should support System Status surfaces for cyberware, suits, drones, locks, turrets, terminals, station modules, ship systems, smart weapons, upload sleeves, and other tech-bearing objects.

Useful visual families include:

- online/offline/fault/degraded/locked/overheated states.
- hackable vs analog surfaces.
- shielded vs exposed systems.
- authorized vs unauthorized access.
- EMP, interference, burn-through, and lockout states.

### Defense surface compatibility

Interface and sheet art should be able to display or support the current defensive spine:

`Defense | Shield | Armor | HP | Firewall`

Icons should work across character sheets, TacMap overlays, and quick reference aids when possible. Equipment owns gear rules and Art owns visual language and asset guidance.
