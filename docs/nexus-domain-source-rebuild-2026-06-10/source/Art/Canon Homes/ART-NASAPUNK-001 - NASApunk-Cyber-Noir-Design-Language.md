# NASApunk Cyber Noir Design Language

## NASApunk layer

NASApunk means grounded spaceflight and habitat design treated as a working industrial system:

- white, beige, grey, and orange paneling;
- pressure doors and hatch indicators;
- EVA rails, clipped tools, and visible tethers;
- thermal blankets, insulation wraps, and patchwork repairs;
- labels, part numbers, hazard tags, and maintenance warnings;
- docking hardware, clamps, handholds, umbilicals, and cargo nets;
- realistic station volumes: narrow service corridors, maintenance crawlspaces, sealed compartments, recycling systems, pressure bulkheads.

## Cyber noir layer

Cyber noir adds the human and political density:

- surveillance cameras and access control;
- cheap augmented displays and old corporate kiosks;
- biometric readers, credential gates, and compromised records;
- bodies marked by implants, grafts, repair scars, and medical economies;
- neon, but sparing and dirty, not glossy arcade neon;
- black-market modifications beside bureaucratic tags and official safety signage.

## Diesel grit layer

Diesel grit gives weight and age:

- thick hinges, heavy valves, old compressors, industrial fans;
- chipped enamel labels and painted hazard stripes;
- soot, frost, condensation, oil sheen, dust, and rust-colored stains;
- retrofitted panels and mixed-generation hardware;
- analog backups beside digital diagnostics.

## Interface style

Interfaces should feel functional and maintenance-oriented:

- flat panels, status tiles, terminal readouts, small warning LEDs;
- simple icons, labels, and callouts;
- gridless tactical overlays where needed;
- amber/teal diagnostic tones;
- limited holography unless a specific technology justifies it;
- no elegant transparent fantasy UI as default.

## Contact / Choir visual language

Contact visuals should be eerie because they are misfit signals inside human systems:

- synchronized error lights;
- medical monitors producing impossible patterns;
- comms noise that seems structured;
- repeated sensor ghosts;
- implanted minds showing shared signal artifacts;
- ordinary maintenance displays behaving as if they are receiving instructions.

Avoid making the Choir look like a normal faction logo or a fantasy hive mind symbol unless a later lore source approves it.

## Faction and culture marks

Faction identities should be utility-first and institutional before decorative:

- agency stamps;
- inspection tags;
- station badges;
- corporate maintenance decals;
- medical quarantine marks;
- field-painted unit symbols;
- public-facing propaganda overlays.

Do not over-finalize faction logos until Lore faction work matures.
## TacMap/collage extension - 2026-05-15

TacMap and diagram assets should translate NASApunk cyber-noir into a schematic language:

- industrial hazard-stencil geometry.
- thick simple linework.
- utilitarian signage and warning shapes.
- exposed mechanical infrastructure used as visual context.
- readable schematic overlays rather than ornamental UI clutter.
- worn, practical, maintenance-heavy motifs even when reduced to icons.

### Icon and marker tone

Use compact practical shapes that feel like signage, maintenance glyphs, hard-suit labels, industrial warning tags, or pressure-system schematics. The icon language should feel like it could exist inside the Nexus setting, but it must still function as a tabletop play aid.
