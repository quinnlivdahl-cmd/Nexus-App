# Lore Domain Readme

## Purpose

The `Lore` domain owns setting, factions, locations, timeline, personhood, station life, Signal/Contact mystery, naming style, and lore open questions.

Lore answers: **what is true about the world, its history, its people, its institutions, and its mysteries?**

## Authority Boundary

Lore owns setting truth and world-facing meaning.

Lore does not own:

- character-facing origin/chassis mechanics;
- cyberware, equipment implementation, EMP, Firewall, or System Status mechanics;
- active campaign state until promoted;
- DM display procedure;
- content payload frameworks;
- reference reports unless promoted;
- final names when a doc marks them as placeholders.

## Current Structure

Current Lore surfaces include:

- `LORE-INDEX-000`: domain readme.
- `LORE-INDEX-001`: domain manifest.
- `LORE-SETTING-001`: setting overview and themes.
- `LORE-PERSONHOOD-001`: humanity, posthumanity, and origin frame.
- `LORE-FACTION-001`: factions, polities, and power structures.
- `LORE-LOCATION-001`: locations, solar system, and travel.
- `LORE-TIMELINE-001`: timeline and historical eras.
- `LORE-SIGNAL-001`: alien Signal and Contact mystery.
- `LORE-STATION-001`: corporate, state, and station life.
- `LORE-CULTURE-001`: naming, language, and cultural style.
- `LORE-OPEN-010`: lore open questions.

## Currentness and Caution

Lore is provisional canon where its docs state working truth, but it is not final locked lore.

Preserve:

- placeholder names;
- unresolved authority names;
- campaign-derived lore as evidence until promoted;
- Signal/Choir mystery pacing;
- reference guidance that may inform future drafting.

Do not collapse campaign facts, seed ideas, or reference scaffolding into global canon without an explicit promotion decision.

## Cross-Domain Routing

- `Characters`: playable origin/chassis implications and character-facing identity systems.
- `Equipment`: cyberware implementation, EMP, gear, Firewall/System Status, and equipment-side rules.
- `Content`: reusable faction/enemy/job payloads and encounter-ready content.
- `Dashboards`: active campaign state and current session facts.
- `Reference`: lore research, comparison, and external plausibility support.
- `Seed`: exploratory lore branches not ready for canon.

## Preservation Rule

No old lore doc, faction doc, timeline doc, handoff, dashboard, seed branch, backup, or broad lore bible is deletion-approved by this readme.
