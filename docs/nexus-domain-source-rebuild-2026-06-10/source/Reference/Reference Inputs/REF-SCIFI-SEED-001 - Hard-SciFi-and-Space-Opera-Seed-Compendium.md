## How To Use This Document

Seed Mode should use this document as a **combinatorics engine**, not as a canon lock. The most useful workflow is to pull one item from several categories at once: one posthuman identity dispute, one infrastructure constraint, one polity model, one ideological axis, and one mystery frame. That combination is usually enough to generate a mission, a recruit, a conflict, a TacMap, and at least one long-term campaign consequence.

Use it to:

- mine for ideas quickly
- cross-pollinate with active Nexus docs

- generate â€œwhy this mechanic exists in-worldâ€
- generate â€œwhat kind of play this lore impliesâ€
- identify where a cool idea must be constrained to stay gameable

Seed Mode should assume that **every major lore idea has a play cost**. If a technology changes travel, ask what it does to route choice, time pressure, logistics, and faction leverage. If a body type changes survival, ask how that affects recruiting, equipment, status, prejudice, and law. If an institution controls air, compute, docking, or substrate time, it is not just scenery; it is a mission generator.

This document should **never override Active or Final docs**. If a live Nexus document already establishes a fact, this compendium exists to enrich that fact, not replace it.

## Classification Key

This compendium uses **hard-SF** in the narrow sense of ideas anchored in established or carefully extrapolated science, and **space opera** in the sense of large-scale, wonder-driven, high-dramatic extrapolation. - **[Grounded]**: Strongly anchored in current science, engineering practice, law, or near-term policy.
- **[Plausible]**: Requires extrapolation, scaling, or social adoption beyond today, but stays within recognizable scientific or institutional logic.
- **[Speculative]**: Not impossible, but dependent on major breakthroughs, new social orders, or unresolved philosophical questions.
- **[Space Opera]**: Intentionally dramatized, larger-than-life, or infrastructure-heavy wonder tech that should still obey local rules once introduced.
- **[Mythic / Symbolic]**: Best used as cultural, religious, psychological, or thematic truth rather than literal technical explanation.

## Part IV â€” Factions and Ideological Axes

The following axes are better than fixed factions when building a living solar system. They let Seed Mode generate many different organizations from the same underlying argument. Each side should be playable, sympathetic, and capable of both genuine principle and ugly extremism.

### Contact and Isolation

- **Contact side:** curiosity, civilizational growth, trade, shared meaning, and moral duty to know.
- **Isolation side:** quarantine, information hazards, asymmetry, and fear that curiosity is a species-level trap.
- **Extremist forms:** beacon cults and forced broadcasters; silence regimes that disappear listeners.
- **Moderate compromises:** monitored listening, layered verification, no unilateral reply authority.
- **Playable missions:** protect a listening array, raid a rogue transmitter, escort a public hearing.
- **Recruitment effects:** seekers, paranoids, linguists, quarantine hardliners.

### Baseline Humanity and Posthuman Expansion

- **Baseline side:** shared standards, mutual recognition, and resistance to caste-by-body.
- **Expansion side:** adaptation is survival; refusing change in space is nostalgia with a body count.
- **Extremist forms:** purity law versus compulsory enhancement.
- **Moderate compromises:** rights floors plus transparent edit licensing and anti-discrimination law.
- **Playable missions:** rescue children from forced-edit clinic, defend a baseline-only monastery, arbitrate edit insurance.
- **Recruitment effects:** cultural conservatives, radical morphologists, body-rights campaigners.

- **Biological side:** embodiment, metabolism, and mortal continuity matter; a copy is a descendant, not you.

- **Discipline side:** rationing, restraint, and duty keep people alive.
- **Liberation side:** abundance should free people from inherited coercions, not just make elites softer.
- **Extremist forms:** punitive austerity cults versus reckless anti-accounting futurists.

### Terraforming and Preservation

- **Terraforming side:** unused worlds are failed opportunities; life should spread.
- **Preservation side:** sterile or alien environments may have value we barely understand.
- **Extremist forms:** ecocidal bloom pushers versus anti-settlement absolutists.

## Part IX â€” Design Warnings

### Too Much Realism Reducing Fun

- **Risk:** logistics smother wonder and pacing.
- **Mitigation:** use realism to create choices and pressure, not accounting homework.

### Too Much Space Opera Breaking Coherence

- **Risk:** every problem gets solved by the next miracle device.
- **Mitigation:** every space-opera element needs infrastructure, cost, constituency, and failure modes.

### Posthuman Ideas Becoming Fantasy Races Without Social Consequences

- **Risk:** the setting betrays its own theme.
- **Mitigation:** tie every body difference to law, labor, kinship, medicine, prejudice, and habitat access.

### Technology Solving Every Problem

- **Risk:** tension evaporates and specialists stop mattering.
- **Mitigation:** make every technology partial, biased, expensive, and politically owned.

### AI Becoming Either Magic or Furniture

- **Risk:** AI is either omnipotent nonsense or useless scenery.
- **Mitigation:** give AI clear domains, dependencies, rights questions, and blind spots.

### Factions Becoming One-Note

- **Risk:** politics collapses into cartoon morality.
- **Mitigation:** build factions from axes so every side has a legitimate fear, a genuine virtue, and an ugly temptation.

### Alien Mystery Being Overexplained

- **Risk:** wonder collapses into wiki trivia.
- **Mitigation:** reveal effects faster than causes, and interpretations faster than truth.

### Mechanics and Lore Disconnect

- **Risk:** rules feel arbitrary and setting flavor feels decorative.
- **Mitigation:** every major mechanic should answer â€œwhat social system makes this believable?â€ and every major lore idea should answer â€œwhat play loop does this create?â€

### Grimdark Flattening Player Agency

- **Risk:** nothing matters except survival and cruelty.
- **Mitigation:** preserve room for solidarity, reform, love, competence, humor, and genuine civic courage.
