# Game Inspiration and Comparable Systems

## Reference status

This doc consolidates comparable-system and inspiration-facing material from the reference family. It is non-authoritative unless a finding is later promoted into a rebuilt source domain.

## Primary takeaways for Nexus source work

- Nexus should remain tabletop-first while learning from video-game and board-game structures.
- FTL-like route pressure, XCOM-like tactical missions, recruit/team collection, and space-D&D character depth are useful comparison lenses, not design obligations.
- Research and inspiration material should be used to ask better design questions, not to import full systems wholesale.
- Hard-scifi and space-opera references can support tone, plausibility, and design options, but active lore decisions remain in `Lore`.
- RNG and balance appendices belong as tuning evidence until exact mechanics are finalized.

## Comparable-system routing

- Combat/TacMap comparisons route to `Combat`.
- Skill/progression comparisons route to `Skills` and `Characters`.
- Loadout, tools, and cyberware comparisons route to `Equipment`.
- Lore/timeline/worldbuilding comparisons route to `Lore`.
- Enemy/content comparisons route to `Content`.
- Automation/app comparisons route to `Automation`.
- Auxiliary-display comparisons route to `Play Aids` or `Dashboards`, depending on use.
- Data/workbook implications route to `Data`.
- Art/style references route to `Art`.
