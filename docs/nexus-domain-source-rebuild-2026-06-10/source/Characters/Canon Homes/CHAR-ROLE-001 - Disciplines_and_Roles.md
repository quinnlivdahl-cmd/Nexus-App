# Disciplines and Roles

## 1. Active frame

Nexus uses disciplines and crew roles rather than fixed fantasy classes. A discipline should describe what a character tends to do under pressure, not lock the character into a class lane.

## 2. Current role signals

Current role language remains provisional. Useful role signals include:

| Role signal | What it contributes |
|---|---|
| Vanguard | Positioning pressure, breach, protection, close-range commitment. |
| Ghost | Stealth, bypass, ambush, extraction, evasion. |
| Operator | Systems, drones, command, battlefield coordination. |
| Medic | Recovery, stabilization, biotech, triage, morale. |
| Envoy | Social access, negotiation, deception, crew/faction leverage. |
| Engineer | Repair, fabrication, equipment adaptation, ship/field systems. |
| Gunner | Firepower, suppression, heavy weapons, area control. |
| Adaptant | Body-driven adaptation, environmental leverage, posthuman edge. |

These are not final classes.

## 3. Chassis and discipline interaction

A body/origin suggests affordances, but discipline decides reliable play pattern.

Examples:

- Ape/gorilla frame + Vanguard leans into breach/load-bearing/body-blocking.
- Ape/gorilla frame + Envoy creates an outsider/social-pressure character instead of only a strong combatant.
- Upload Maintenance Drone + Engineer leans into repair/access.
- Upload Maintenance Drone + Ghost leans into infiltration and duct work.
- Cybernetically integrated character + Operator may emphasize dronejack/neural-port play.
- Splicer + Medic may emphasize biotech, rejection management, or living-body expertise.

## 4. DM Mode use

When a player proposes an action, DM Mode should ask:

```text
Is this action enabled by body/origin, discipline training, skill/focus, cyberware, equipment, or crew help?
```

If multiple layers support it, the character is specialized and should feel strong. If only body shape supports it, allow the fiction but avoid granting free mastery.

## 5. Boundaries

Discipline mechanics, skill focuses, and selectable techniques cross into `Skills`. Gear and cyberware trees cross into `Equipment`. This doc keeps the role vocabulary usable while final advancement math remains open.

## 6. Body/chassis and discipline interaction

Disciplines should make body/chassis affordances reliable, but should not erase body tradeoffs.

Working guidance:

- a body may provide an opportunity, permission, or pressure;
- a discipline, skill, feature, gear item, cyberware choice, or crew support makes that opportunity dependable;
- disciplines can specialize recovery, repair, continuity, body use, or system stabilization later;
- discipline design should not assume every origin/body has the same advancement path.

Examples:

- an ape/gorilla body may have strong shove/carry presence, but Vanguard/Gunner/Engineer choices decide how that becomes reliable tactical value;
- a Vatborn may resist panic or intimidation, but discipline/skill choices decide how that focus becomes leadership, violence, stealth, labor, or technical play;
- a heavy cyberware build may gain tactical tools, but discipline and gear determine whether the hack-surface and maintenance costs are worth it.
