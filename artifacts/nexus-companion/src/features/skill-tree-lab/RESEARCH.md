# Skill Tree Lab research notes

The bundled Research Library compares 16 games across tabletop RPGs, CRPGs, tactical games, immersive sims, and science-fiction games, plus six fiction-derived capability lenses. Each record keeps the source, observed pattern, strength, risk, Nexus adaptation, avoid note, original candidate application, and a direct citation.

The synthesis recommends a bounded concrete middle layer for the candidate tree:

- Attributes remain the six playable-draft labels from Issue 31.
- Skills own coherent families of repeatable checks, such as Firearms, Medicine, and Computing.
- Broad concepts such as offense, defense, command, and support remain coverage lenses unless later source review promotes them as structural parents.
- Skill Focuses carry character fantasy and distinct play patterns.
- Abilities grant clear verbs, permissions, reactions, stances, preparations, or equipment interactions.
- OR prerequisites support ordinary alternate routes. AND prerequisites are reserved for genuine synthesis.
- Baseline survival and required-content access should not be hidden behind tax purchases.

The bundled tree has been promoted to playtest-ready provisional source and remains editable. Individual entries still carry proposal status so playtest findings can revise them without implying final balance. The Nexus Rules Map separately labels existing rules, prior proposals, and open questions.
